\section*{Problem 6}
\begin{eqnarray*}
    y &=& \frac{1}{2}(x + 3) \\
   2y &=& (x + 3) \\
x + 3 &=& 2y \\
    x &=& 2y - 3
\end{eqnarray*}

Suppose $ y $ is increased by 1 so that $ y' = y + 1 $, then
\begin{eqnarray*}
  x' - x &=& 2y' - 3 - (2y - 3) \\
         &=& 2y' - 2y \\
         &=& 2(y' - y) \\
         &=& 2
\end{eqnarray*}
Therefore whenever $ y $ is increased by 1, $ x $ is increased by 2.
